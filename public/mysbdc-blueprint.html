The strategy

One unified portal — on purpose.

The revised direction drops regional site-switching entirely. Clients land on one fast, network-wide front door. Local connection still happens — just quietly, in the background, where it belongs.

Today — 18 fragmented experiences
  • A different website, look, and login for every region
  • Clients repeat their information at every step — the 641 is a chore
  • Events scattered across sites; registration handled by hand
  • Reporting assembled manually from spreadsheets, weeks later
  • Search-engine authority diluted across 18 separate domains
MySBDC — one portal
  • One website, one brand: “SBDC: Your business. Better.”
  • One account works everywhere — sign in once with Google, Microsoft, or email
  • Every training in one calendar with true one-click registration
  • Attendance, milestones, and impact reporting build themselves
  • All authority concentrated on a single domain: norcalsbdc.org
Removed on purpose: ZIP-code finders, “is this your center?” suggestion banners, “Nearby Today” prompts, and header logos that flip by region. Instead, the intake form captures each client’s business ZIP once and quietly routes them to the right Success Team behind the scenes. Clients never have to think about org structure — they just get the right people.

The golden rule: one source of truth per data type

Every fact lives in exactly one system, and the website simply shows it — it never keeps a competing copy. That single rule is why nothing drifts out of sync, why staff stop re-typing the same information into three places, and why the reports are always current.

1 · Browse one calendar

Every training across the entire network, in one place, with directory-style filters: topic, date, format, price. No more 18 different event pages.

Behind the curtain

Staff create each event once, in Neoserra. The website notices new events overnight and publishes them automatically — virtual events even get their Zoom room created for them. Nobody re-types an event onto a website ever again.

2 · One click, zero forms

Because Maria is already signed in, the portal knows who she is. Registering is literally one click — name, email, and business details come along automatically.

Behind the curtain

The moment she clicks, two things happen at once: the live classroom (Zoom) reserves her seat, and her client file (Neoserra) is marked “Registered.” For paid sessions, a secure checkout (Stripe) appears first — one step, receipt by email.

3 · Instant confirmation

A personal join link — unique to Maria — plus add-to-calendar buttons. The link is also saved to her dashboard, so losing the email doesn’t matter.

Behind the curtain

The personalized link is what makes attendance tracking automatic later: when Maria joins through it, the system knows it’s her — no sign-in sheet needed.

4 · My Trainings — only hers

The dashboard shows just what Maria registered for: upcoming live sessions and in-progress courses. On-demand courses open instantly with the same login — no second password.

Behind the curtain

This list isn’t a copy — it’s a live view of her registrations in Zoom and her course progress in LearnWorlds. One source of truth, displayed in one place.

5 · Day of: just “Join now”

No meeting IDs, no passcodes, no digging through a crowded inbox. The button lights up when it’s time.

Behind the curtain

The button uses her personal join link — which is also how her attendance gets recorded without anyone lifting a finger.

6 · Afterwards: the loop closes itself

This is the step nobody sees — and the one that saves the most staff time. Attendance flows back to each client’s record automatically, overnight.

Behind the curtain

Every night the portal compares who registered with who actually attended, and updates each client file in Neoserra: Attended or No-show. SBA-compliant tracking, complete, with zero manual entry.

The Smart 641 — a form people actually finish.

The traditional SBA Form 641 is the single biggest source of friction and bad data in the network. MySBDC replaces it with a friendly, autosaving wizard — and uses the answers to give every client the right start, automatically.

Question 4 of 10✓ Saved automatically
How soon are you hoping to access capital?
Within the next 90 days 3–6 months Just exploring for now
One question per screen, plain language, smart branching — questions that don’t apply never appear. Stop anytime; it picks up where you left off.
73Readiness
Business is registered and operating Financial documents mostly ready Seeking capital within 90 days
A readiness score (0–100) is calculated on submission from status, document readiness, capital timeline, and credit signals. Clients never see homework — just the right next step.
Advising-ready score ≥ 60

Auto-paired with an advisor and invited to a 15-minute Success Team Welcome Call — white-glove from minute one.

Training-first score < 60

Self-serve starter-track modules build readiness; the dashboard nudges them back to advising when they’re ready.

Fast-track automatic 100

Launching in <90 days, or referred by a lender? Straight to red-carpet advisor access — no waiting.

Two tracks plus a fast lane. The business ZIP captured here quietly maps each client to the right Success Team — no region-picking by the client, ever.
