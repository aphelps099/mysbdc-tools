Clear Space

Minimum clear space equal to the height of the “S” in SBDC around all sides.

Do

Use provided files exactly as supplied. Place on approved backgrounds. Maintain clear space. Star mark for small applications (favicons, pins).

Don’t

Stretch, rotate, or recolor. Place on busy photos without a color block. Add shadows, outlines, or effects. Recreate with other typefaces.

Guidelines
Each rule includes a rationale — the “why,” not just the “what.”

Color Usage

Do

Navy for dark backgrounds. Pool for interactive accents. Cream for light sections. White text on Navy or Royal. Navy text on Cream or Pool.

Don’t

White text on Pool or Cream — fails accessibility. Royal as full-page background. Pool and Royal as adjacent elements. Colors outside the five-color palette.

Typography Hierarchy

Do

Tobias Regular for editorial headlines — the serif form carries authority without needing bold. GT America Light (300) for body. Bold (700) only on tiny uppercase labels. Mono for stats and metadata.

Don’t

Use Tobias below 20px or in bold weights (the form carries itself). Mix more than two families on a single component. Use bold GT America for body or headlines. Mono for body copy.

Photography

Do

Real NorCal clients and communities. Natural light, authentic settings. Diversity of geography — urban, rural, agricultural. Slight warm grade for consistency.

Don’t

Generic stock that doesn’t reflect Northern California. Over-filtered or heavily stylized. Logo on busy photos without a color block or overlay.

Type by Channel

Social Media
Tobias Regular for headline. GT America for supporting text. Pool accent for key stats. Max 2 lines for legibility at feed size. Navy background preferred.
Presentations
Tobias for slide titles. GT America for body. Mono for section labels. One idea per slide. Navy backgrounds for impact, Cream for data slides.
Email
GT America as primary (web-safe fallback). 16px body minimum. Royal CTA buttons on cream or white. Subject lines under 50 characters.
Print
Tobias for posters and banners. GT America for brochure body. Mono for wayfinding. Minimum 10pt body. Use CMYK conversions from guide.

Messaging
How we sound matters as much as how we look. Four attributes define the NorCal SBDC voice.
Expert, not academic
We translate SBA programs, capital markets, and business fundamentals into plain, actionable language. No jargon walls.
Warm, not casual
Encouraging and human, but never unprofessional. Think trusted advisor at a coffee meeting, not a text from a friend.
Confident, not boastful
$547M in capital accessed. 20 years. 36 counties. We let the numbers speak. Clear facts, no overselling.
Action-oriented
Every message points toward a next step. “Your business, better” isn’t just a tagline — it’s a promise that we meet you where you are and move you forward.
