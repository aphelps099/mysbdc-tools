COMPLEXITY ISSUES

1

4-5 Text Hierarchy Levels

America's → SBDC → State → Region → Center. The eye doesn't know where to land first.

2

3-Color Minimum Requirement

Navy, red bar, and often a third for regional distinction. Fails in single-color applications.

3

Red Bar Creates Visual Noise

The divider bar adds visual weight without adding meaning. It fragments rather than unifies.

4

Doesn't Scale Down

At favicon/social avatar sizes (32×32px), the text becomes illegible mud.

5

Centers Add MORE Complexity

Each center then adds their host institution logo, creating 6-7 element lockups.

04 / 07

The Pairing System

A simplified mark enables two elegant ways to denote center locations—without adding visual chaos back into the logo.

Option A: Text Callout

Recommended
Marin SBDC
at College of Marin

A thin 1px vertical bar separates the network mark from the center name. The center's host institution appears as a secondary line. Clean, scalable, consistent.

Option B: Host Logo Pairing

For Co-Branding
University / College Logo

When host institution co-branding is required, their logo sits to the right of the separator. Both marks maintain equal visual weight.
