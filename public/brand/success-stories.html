Story Scoring Rubric
Use this rubric to evaluate and classify new client stories. Every story should be scored before publishing.
Criteria Signature (4–5 pts) Growth (2–3 pts) Spotlight (0–1 pts)
Economic Impact $100K+ capital accessed, OR 5+ jobs, OR major revenue growth $10K–$100K capital, OR 1–4 jobs, OR moderate growth Under $10K, or no quantified impact
Structure Clear Challenge → Solution → Results arc with metrics front and center Has the arc but results are buried or text is too long Missing structure, feelings-heavy, or no clear arc
Quote Quality Specific, attributable, mentions SBDC by name, speaks to measurable outcome Positive but generic (“couldn’t have done it without them”) Only emotional / no business substance
Photography Professional headshot or business photography available Has some imagery but not professional No photography available
Reusability Can headline an annual report, board presentation, or lender pitch Works for newsletters, blog posts, partner updates with light editing Best for social media posts and community engagement
Quick Score Guide
20–25 Signature Story — ready for prime time
12–19 Growth Story — needs tightening before formal use
0–11 Community Spotlight — use for social media and community content
Story Toolkit
Templates and guidelines for writing new success stories to the Signature standard.
A. Story Structure Template
Kicker
NorCal SBDC Success Story — appears above the headline, establishes context
Business Name
Large, bold headline — the business name displayed prominently
Tagline
One sentence describing what the business does — clear and concise
Hero Metric
The single most impressive number, displayed large — this is the anchor of the story
Quote
Direct quote from the business owner — specific and mentioning SBDC
The Challenge
2–3 sentences max. What was the obstacle?
The Solution
2–3 sentences max. What did SBDC do specifically?
The Results
Lead with numbers. 3–4 metric boxes displayed prominently.
Partner CTA
Link to the NorCal SBDC center that helped — drives visitors back to the network
B. Writing Rules
Do
Lead with the most impressive metric
Keep the total story under 400 words
Use “no-fee” never “free”
Name the specific NorCal SBDC center and advisor
Include at least 3 quantified outcomes
Get a direct, specific quote about business results
Use Challenge → Solution → Results structure
Don’t
Bury results at the bottom
Write more than 3 sentences per section
Focus on feelings without tangible outcomes
Use generic quotes (“great experience”)
Lead with the business backstory
Forget photography — always capture a portrait
Use “SBDC” without “NorCal” prefix
C. Metric Priority Guide
Capital Accessed $ amount
Jobs Created or Retained headcount
Revenue / Sales Growth $ or %
Loans Secured $ amount + lender
Cost Reduction %
Certifications Obtained WBE, MBE, etc.
Clients / Customers Gained headcount
Social / Digital Growth followers, traffic
Stories with metrics ranked 1–4 as primary are almost always Signature-quality. Stories led by metrics 5–8 need supporting capital/jobs data to reach Signature tier.
