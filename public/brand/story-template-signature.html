The Challenge

Rejected by banks. One week to close.

Chris Aguirre had worked at Marin Auto Works for years as a technician. When the owner decided to sell, Chris saw the opportunity of a lifetime — but lenders saw a technician, not a business owner.

Traditional banks turned him down flat. No business plan. No financial projections. No lending relationships. And the existing owner was ready to walk — Chris had less than a week to finalize the acquisition or lose the shop.

“I thought getting a loan would be easy — like in the movies, where you walk into a bank and boom, you get a loan. After being rejected by the big banks, I was referred to the SBDC.”

Chris Aguirre
Owner, Marin Auto Works
Business Interior / Exterior
The Solution

Expert guidance through the SBA labyrinth.

A Marin SBDC advisor stepped in immediately, helping Chris build a complete SBA loan package in record time — financial projections, business plan, and the right lending program for an acquisition this size.

The advisor's deep relationships with local lenders proved critical, connecting Chris directly with decision-makers who understood the value of a technician-to-owner transition. What seemed impossible became a done deal.
